Welcome to the Battle System Tutorial

This tutorial will guide you through the mechanics of the battle system. Use the arrows in the bottom right to navigate through the slides.

Battle System Overview

In the battle system, players take turns selecting moves for their Pokémon to attack the opponent.

  • Each Pokémon has a set of moves based on its type.
  • Moves consume stamina, so manage your resources wisely.
  • Special moves are exclusive to certain Pokémon and can turn the tide of battle.
  • Victory is achieved when all of the opponent's Pokémon are defeated.

How to Select Moves

Follow these steps to select a move during your turn:

  1. Choose a move from the list displayed on your side of the Battlefield.
  2. Confirm your selection to execute the move.

Tip: Pay attention to move types and stamina costs to maximize your strategy!

Pokémon Types and Advantages

Understanding type advantages is crucial for success in battles:

  • Fire is strong against Grass but weak against Water.
  • Water is strong against Fire but weak against Grass.
  • Grass is strong against Water but weak against Fire.

Tip: Use type advantages to deal extra damage to your opponent's Pokémon! Look up the Type Advantages here!

Special Moves

Special moves are exclusive to certain Pokémon and can provide a significant advantage:

  • Phantom Slash: A Ghost-type move exclusive to Aegislash, Gengar, and Haunter.
  • Molten Sky Strike: A Fire-type move exclusive to Charizard and Blaziken.
  • Titanic Tremor: A Rock-type move exclusive to Geodude, Onix, Steelix, and Rhyhorn.

Tip: Build your team around Pokémon with powerful special moves!

Stamina Management

Each move consumes stamina. If your Pokémon runs out of stamina, it won't be able to attack:

  • Weaker moves consume less stamina and can be used more frequently.
  • Powerful moves consume more stamina but deal higher damage.
  • Plan your stamina usage carefully to avoid running out during critical moments.

Building Your Team

When building your team, consider the following:

  • Choose Pokémon with complementary types to cover weaknesses.
  • Include a mix of offensive and defensive Pokémon.
  • Take advantage of trainer perks to enhance your strategy.

Tip: Experiment with different team compositions to find what works best for you!

Pokémon Selector

The Pokémon Selector allows you to choose the Pokémon for your team. Follow these steps:

  1. Click on a Pokémon from the list to view its details.
  2. Review its stats, type, and available moves.
  3. Click on the image of the Pokémon to open its detailed Selector
  4. Use the arrows to switch between Pokémon options.
  5. Click "Save" to confirm your selection.

Tip: Build a balanced team with complementary types to cover weaknesses!

Pokémon Details

When selecting a Pokémon, you can view its detailed stats:

  • HP: The Pokémon's health points.
  • Stamina: Determines how many moves the Pokémon can use.
  • Might: The Pokémon's attack power.
  • Resistance: The Pokémon's defense against attacks.
  • Speed: Determines which Pokémon attacks first.

Tip: Choose Pokémon with high stats that match your strategy!

Move Selector

The Move Selector allows you to customize your Pokémon's moves. Here's how it works:

  1. Select a Pokémon to view its available moves.
  2. Click on a move to see its details, including power, accuracy, and stamina cost.
  3. Choose a move slot and assign the selected move to it.
  4. Click "Save" to confirm your move selection.

Tip: Use moves that complement your Pokémon's type and strategy!

Move Details

When selecting a move, you can view its detailed stats:

  • Power: The damage the move deals.
  • Accuracy: The chance of the move hitting the opponent.
  • Stamina Cost: The stamina required to use the move.
  • Description: Special effects or conditions of the move.

Tip: Balance powerful moves with low-cost moves to manage stamina effectively!

Trainer Selector

The Trainer Selector lets you choose a trainer with unique perks to enhance your strategy:

  1. Click on a trainer to view their perk.
  2. Use the arrows to switch between trainers.
  3. Choose a trainer that complements your team's strengths and weaknesses.
  4. Click "Save" to confirm your trainer selection.

Tip: Trainer perks can turn the tide of battle, so choose wisely!

Trainer Perks

Each trainer has a unique perk that can influence the battle:

  • Fire-Trainer: Fiery Rebirth - Revives the first fainted Fire Pokémon at half health.
  • Water-Trainer: Torrential Boost - Water moves gain 20% power when HP is below 50%.
  • Grass-Trainer: Verdant Recovery - Grass Pokémon regain 5% HP at the end of each turn.

Choose your trainer wisely to complement your team!

Victory Conditions

To win a battle, you must defeat all of your opponent's Pokémon:

  • Use type advantages to deal extra damage.
  • Manage your stamina and resources effectively.
  • Adapt your strategy based on your opponent's moves.
